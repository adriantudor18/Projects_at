NBA Career

  • Drafted: LeBron was the 1st overall pick in the 2003 NBA Draft by the Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • Cleveland Cavaliers (2003–2010): LeBron quickly became one of the league's brightest stars, winning Rookie of the Year. During his first stint with Cleveland, he earned 2 MVP awards (2009, 2010) and led the team to the NBA Finals in 2007, though they lost to the San Antonio Spurs.
  • Miami Heat (2010–2014): In 2010, LeBron made a controversial decision to join the Miami Heat, forming a "superteam" with Dwyane Wade and Chris Bosh. During his four years with Miami, he won two NBA championships (2012, 2013) and earned 2 more MVP awards (2012, 2013).
  • Return to Cleveland (2014–2018): LeBron returned to the Cavaliers in 2014 and led them to the 2016 NBA Championship, delivering Cleveland’s first-ever title and breaking the city’s 52-year championship drought. He also earned Finals MVP honors for his performance.
  • Los Angeles Lakers (2018–Present): LeBron signed with the Lakers in 2018. In 2020, he led the Lakers to their 17th championship, tying them with the Boston Celtics for the most in NBA history. He was named Finals MVP.

Off the Court

Off the court, LeBron James is highly influential, using his platform for philanthropy, activism, and business. Through his LeBron James Family Foundation, he has made a significant impact on education, most notably with the opening of the I PROMISE School in Akron for at-risk youth, and by providing scholarships to students. He is also a vocal advocate for social justice, racial equality, and voting rights, supporting initiatives like More Than a Vote to combat voter suppression. In business, LeBron co-founded SpringHill Company, a media venture producing films and television, including the 2021 "Space Jam" movie. LeBron’s contributions go far beyond the basketball court.
